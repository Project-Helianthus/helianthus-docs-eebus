# Protected Outbound Pairing Attempt - Partial/Negative

- Evidence id: `EV-20260720-001`
- Evidence class: `observed_runtime`
- Capture date: not published
- Runtime class: Home Assistant add-on on the local LAN
- Device role: private selected outbound-pairing target; no public endpoint or identity
- Acquisition method: independent Home Assistant Core Zeroconf observation
- Artifact class: `shareable_redacted`
- Private artifact retained: no
- Redaction mode: contract facts and ordinal outcome only; no report digest
- Publication status: publishable

## Observed Attempt

An independent observer resolved the local Helianthus `SHIP` pairing service for one
protected outbound-pairing attempt. With first trust closed, the TXT record
exposed `register=false`. During one authenticated bounded window, the same
service exposed `register=true` while handshake auto-accept remained disabled.
Closing or expiring the window returned the local state to pairing closed.

The attempt was partial and negative: no completed pairing candidate, `SHIP`
session, OOB confirmation, persistent trust write, or trusted reconnect was
observed. The observer did not publish, retain, or infer a target endpoint,
address, port, remote identity, or protocol-service identity. This evidence
supports only the local registration transition and its separation from
automatic acceptance. It does
not establish reachability, an initiator/responder role, peer identity, a
trusted VR940f session, SPINE data, semantic support, or behavior outside this
attempt.

No report digest or private artifact is retained or published for this
partial/negative public summary.
